Snorkeling Stops!

Where we snorkel.

Hol Chan Marine Reserve

The Hol Chan marine reserve near Ambergis Caye and Caye Caulker is Belize's first marine protected area, established in 1987. Known for its diverse marine ecosystems, it features coral reefs, seagrass beds, mangroves, and the famous Shark Ray Alley, where visitors can snorkel with nurse sharks and stingrays. Hol Chan, meaning "Little Channel" in Maya, offers incredible opportunities to explore vibrant underwater life while promoting marine conservation and sustainable tourism.

Caye Caulker South Channel

The Caye Caulker South Channel is a picturesque and serene area within the Caye Caulker Marine Reserve in Belize. Known for its crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life, this channel is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving. It features stunning coral formations and provides opportunities to observe tropical fish, sea turtles, and other fascinating underwater creatures.

Coral Gardens

The Coral Gardens in Caye Caulker's Marine Reserve is a beautiful snorkeling site with shallow, clear waters, vibrant coral formations, and diverse marine life like tropical fish and sea turtles. It's ideal for both beginners and experienced snorkelers, often visited as part of guided tours exploring the area's natural wonders.
